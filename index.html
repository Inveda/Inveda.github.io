The Gift of the Magi is a short story, written by O. Henry (a pen name for William Sydney Porter), about a young husband and wife and how they deal with the challenge of buying secret Christmas gifts for each other with very little money. 

Introduction

It is very evident that the couple is suffering from poverty but because of their love for each other they did everything up to the extent of their ability to give each other a gift. This story is about love and sacrifice, it’s a reminder that material things are not really important as long as you both love each other. Thus, even if you give them the smallest thing as long as it’s from the heart, it doesn’t matter at all.

There are those people in the world who think that just by giving materials as gifts would make them happy. Della as a person, was overwhelmed by the value of materialism that she didn’t even think if her husband would appreciate her gift. Victims, like Della and Jim though really hard workers still suffer from this kind of system, buying goods that are beyond their purchasing capacity. The couple is victims who suffer from wants developed their own worlds for the sake of keeping the values set by the society they live in.

Poverty does not allow people to understand how the others derive happiness from what they have in their possession and rather misleads them by inculcating in them the idea that one should own something to derive any pleasure from it. Jim and Della have a sense of belonging towards each other, but in this confusion they leave aside what goes with their own personalities in their mutual relationship.
